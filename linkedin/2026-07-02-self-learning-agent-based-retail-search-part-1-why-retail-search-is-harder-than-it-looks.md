Retail search is harder than it looks because relevance is only the beginning.

In the first article of my new series, Self-Learning Agent-Based Retail Search, I start with a question I use in my graduate Information Retrieval course:

If every returned product is semantically relevant, which one should appear first?

Highest engagement? Biggest discount? Highest margin? Best reviews?

My answer has always been: all of the above.

Retail search is a balancing problem across customer satisfaction, business goals, merchandising, inventory, promotions, trust, and now mobile-first behavior where the first result can occupy most of the screen.

This series will explore what happens when we start with the business goal, then let specialized AI agents improve query understanding, synonyms, normalization, indexing, ranking, and evaluation through measured experiments.
