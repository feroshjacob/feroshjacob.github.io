AI application generation feels new.

But the more I worked with coding agents, the more it reminded me of something software engineering has been exploring for decades: Model-Driven Engineering.

The old idea was simple: humans describe systems at a higher level, and machines generate implementation artifacts from those descriptions.

The AI version is more flexible, but the deeper question is the same: what should be the primary artifact?

I am starting to think the answer is not code, and not even the application model. It is the mission: the intent, constraints, success criteria, and validation rules that define whether the application actually works.

That shift changes how we think about generation, validation, learning, and software quality.
